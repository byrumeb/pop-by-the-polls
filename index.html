Issues

There are many things voters consider when deciding who to vote for in any election. When voting for the next president, those issues are even more important to consider. Below are four issues that have all become hot topics this election season.

COVID-19

As millions across the world are dying from the coronavirus, it is no suprise that COVID-19 remains a large issue for world leaders. How to mitigate the spread, vaccine production, and testing availability are all issues the next presidential candidate will have to handle. While doctors are learning more and more about the virus, it is still spiking in the United States. The virus itself may not be a political issue, but the reaction to it has initiated extensive new government policy across the country and globe.

Environment

As the environment, climate change, and the status of natural resources continue to fluctuate, many wonder how and when policy makers and government leaders will get involved. The environmental debate comes down to protecting the Earth or personal gain. It is necessary to take steps in order to preserve natural resources and the status of the Earth. However, it is frequently debated how to do so, leading to extensive political and policy discussion.

The Supreme Court

With the passing of Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg, it has become highly debated on who the next Supreme Court Justice will be. Many voters are hoping the justice to fill her seat will be appointed after this election in the hopes of leveling the court. President Donald Trump has already appointed two justices, Justice Neil Gorsuch and Justice Brett Kavanaugh. Aside from the highly anticipated appointment, there are many controversial SCOTUS cases on the upcoming docket.

Racism

In light of recent events, systemic racism, equity, and policy brutality have been a hot topic of contention for presidential candidates and voters. Many across the country and world want to see genuine and substantial ongoing change to create and preserve equal opportunity for all Americans. While this change will certainly take time and effort, voters are interested to see how each candidate plans and proves to be an active ally for minority demographics.

Candidate

Joe Biden is the Democratic Party candidate in the 2020 election. Read below for more information.

Joe Biden

Joe Biden is a lifelong politician from Scranton, Pennsylvania. This is his third presidential campaign, following runs in 1988 and 2008. Prior to the 2020 election, he served as a senator for the state of Delaware, spanning six terms. He is the 47th vice president of the United States, serving two terms alongside former President of the United States, Barack Obama.

Polling Place

There are many polling places across the state. However, it is important to know where you are registered to vote before you go. Polling locations depend on your district and precinct. Read below to find out more about polling locations in the North Carolina House District Nine.

The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints is an in-person polling place for constituents of the Greenville precinct #11A. It is located at 307 Martinsborough Rd, Greenville, NC on the corner of Martinsborough and Asbury Roads. Voters may park in the Church Parking lot or along the street. Please remember to wear a mask inside and stay six feet apart from other voters and poll workers.

Sample Ballot

Filling out a ballot can seem stressful at first. Use the sample ballot below to learn more about how to complete your ballot.

This is a sample ballot for NC House District Nine. The grey box above each section of candidates indicates the office they are running for and how many candidates you can select. Once you have decided who to vote for, fill in the bubble next to their name. Bubbles should be filled in completely, using a black, felt-tip pen. Be sure to double check that you vote for every office. Happy voting!
